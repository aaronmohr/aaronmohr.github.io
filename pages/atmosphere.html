Atmosphere

UX/UI Design

Autumn 2016

Atmosphere is an experiment in user interface design. The focus of the project was to explore new ways of presenting data and integrating that within to a digital product. In doing so, I developped a data visualization tactic using basic geometric shapes and colours and applied it to a mobile app that measures the local air quality index.

The visual system is built around the use of shapes representing numerical values.
The number of sides on the shape visually depict the air quality index.

Colour Balance

In addition to shapes, I included to use of colour to further enhance the visual concept, making the information more immediately accessible.

Humidex

The size of the shape represents the humidex on a scale from 0 - 100%.

Timeline

Each shape moves on an x and y axis. The x-axis moves along a timeline of 12 hour integers, shifting between day and night. The x-axis showcase the temperature.

Product Integration

Once the visual system had been developed, the concept was applied to a digital product called Atmosphere. Atmosphere is a mobile application that monitors and compares the air quality across a daily timeline. The developped shapes move accross a timeline, using the visual system to depict the air quality, humidity and temperature.
